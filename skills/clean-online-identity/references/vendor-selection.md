# Vendor Selection

Use this reference when comparing DIY cleanup with paid services such as data removal subscriptions, privacy apps, or managed opt-out services.

## Compare Without Stale Claims

Prices, broker coverage, jurisdictions, scan cadence, cancellation terms, and guarantees change often. Verify current vendor pages before comparing services. Treat vendor marketing claims as vendor claims unless independently verified.

## Decision Criteria

Compare:

- Coverage: people-search sites, marketing brokers, risk brokers, registered data brokers, international coverage, and search-result monitoring.
- Recurrence: one-time removals versus recurring scans and re-removals.
- Evidence: whether the service provides a report of discovered profiles and completed opt-outs.
- Data disclosed: what identity data the vendor needs to act.
- Jurisdiction support: US state rights, California DROP/CCPA, GDPR/UK GDPR, PIPEDA, Australia APP, or other regions.
- Delegation model: whether the vendor acts as authorized agent and what authorization is required.
- Sensitive cases: stalking, minors, public figures, domestic violence, and address confidentiality needs.
- Cancellation and data retention: whether the vendor deletes the user's data after cancellation.

## Recommendations

- Prefer DIY when the case is narrow, urgent, or involves a small number of known source pages.
- Prefer paid recurring services when the user wants ongoing people-search scans across many sites and accepts the data disclosure tradeoff.
- Prefer hybrid workflows when the user has urgent high-risk exposure plus long-tail broker recurrence.
- Do not recommend a vendor solely because it advertises dark-web monitoring; dark-web monitoring does not remove data from criminal markets.
