# Jurisdiction Rights

Use this reference to choose request language and response expectations. Verify current official guidance before giving legal deadlines, filing complaints, or relying on a specific form. Record the checked source in `sources_checked.csv`.

## United States

Use broker opt-out and privacy request paths first. US privacy rights vary by state, business type, data category, and exemptions. Do not imply one national deletion right covers all brokers.

Common US actions:

- People-search opt-out requests for profile removal.
- State privacy requests to delete, access, correct, opt out of sale/sharing, or limit sensitive personal information where available.
- Google Results about you requests for eligible personal contact information in search results.
- Credit freezes with Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.
- Fraud alerts through one of the three credit bureaus when identity theft is suspected.
- IdentityTheft.gov report and recovery plan when misuse occurred.

## California

California DROP is live for California consumers as of January 1, 2026. California residents can use DROP to send a single deletion and opt-out request to registered data brokers after verifying eligibility. Data brokers begin processing DROP requests on August 1, 2026; from that phase, brokers must process requests on the statutory cadence and delete eligible data within the current official timeline.

For California residents:

- Prefer DROP for registered data brokers when it fits the case.
- Still use direct broker opt-outs for urgent exposure, non-registered sites, source pages, and search-result removals.
- Explain that DROP requires California residency verification through the official state flow.
- Track `deadline_basis` as `DROP pre-processing`, `DROP processing`, `direct broker opt-out`, or `CCPA/CPRA request` based on the current official page checked.
- Use `california-drop-ccpa-deletion.md` for direct requests or notes supporting DROP.

## European Union

Use GDPR rights when the requester is in the EU or the controller is otherwise subject to GDPR:

- Access to personal data.
- Rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data.
- Erasure when a valid Article 17 ground applies.
- Restriction of processing in specific cases.
- Objection to processing, including direct marketing.
- Recipient notification when data has been disclosed and the request is valid.

Use `gdpr-uk-erasure-request.md` for erasure and `direct-marketing-objection.md` for marketing suppression. Do not say erasure is absolute. Controllers may refuse or retain data under lawful exemptions and must generally explain refusal and complaint rights.

## United Kingdom

Use UK GDPR and ICO guidance. The right to erasure is not absolute and only applies in certain circumstances, including when data is no longer needed, consent is withdrawn, processing is unlawful, direct marketing is objected to, or legal obligation requires erasure.

UK requests can be verbal or written, but prefer written requests for evidence. Ask for response within one calendar month unless current ICO guidance says otherwise. Note that ICO guidance is under review after the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025, so verify before asserting edge-case rules.

## Canada

Under PIPEDA, many private-sector organizations must provide access to personal information and allow individuals to challenge accuracy and completeness. Use written requests. Organizations generally respond within 30 calendar days, with limited extensions when the organization gives timely notice.

Common Canada actions:

- Written access request.
- Correction or amendment request for incomplete or inaccurate data.
- Request disclosure of how data was used and to whom it was disclosed.
- Request that unresolved correction concerns be recorded.
- Complaint to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada after trying to resolve the issue with the organization.

Use `pipeda-access-correction.md`. Do not overstate a broad PIPEDA deletion right.

## Australia

Australian Privacy Principle 12 gives individuals a right to access personal information held by covered organizations or agencies unless an exception applies. A reasonable response period usually does not exceed 30 calendar days. APP 13 supports correction of inaccurate, out-of-date, incomplete, irrelevant, or misleading personal information.

Common Australia actions:

- Access request to the organization or agency.
- Correction request with evidence.
- Request that a statement be associated with the record if correction is refused.
- Complaint to OAIC if the response is unsatisfactory after trying to resolve it with the organization.

Use `australia-app-access-correction.md`. Do not promise deletion as a general Australian privacy right.
